# Saverys Brand Voice

## Tone

Quiet confidence. Every word is chosen, never filler. The voice sounds like someone who has nothing to prove because the work speaks for itself.

Think: a handwritten note on heavy card stock, not a marketing email.

## Principles

1. **Understated over emphatic.** Never use exclamation marks. Never use "amazing", "stunning", "luxury" (the word itself is never luxurious). Let the photography and the restraint communicate quality.

2. **Specific over generic.** "Hand-finished English linen" rather than "high-quality fabrics". Name materials, places, and processes.

3. **Calm over urgent.** No urgency-driven language. No "Book now", "Don't miss out", "Limited availability". Time moves slowly here.

4. **Second person, sparingly.** Address the reader as "you" only when it deepens connection. Otherwise, describe the work in third person or let it exist on its own.

5. **Short sentences. Then shorter ones.** Paragraphs are 2–3 sentences maximum. Let whitespace do the work between thoughts.

## Heading Style

- Sentence case (not Title Case, not ALL CAPS in body — only navigation and buttons use uppercase).
- Headings are evocative, not descriptive. "A room that remembers" rather than "Our design philosophy".
- Maximum 6 words per heading where possible.

## Sample Copy

### Homepage Hero
"Rooms composed with care."

### About — Opening
"Saverys is an interior design studio rooted in the Cotswolds. We work with those who understand that true quality is felt before it is seen."

### Services — Introduction
"Every project begins with listening. We take the time to understand how you live, what you value, and what a room should feel like before a single fabric is chosen."

### Contact — Heading
"Begin a conversation"

### Button Labels
- "View the collection" (not "See all projects")
- "Get in touch" (not "Contact us now")
- "Learn more" (not "Read more")

## Words to Avoid

- Luxury, luxurious
- Stunning, amazing, beautiful
- Bespoke (overused)
- World-class
- Exclusive
- Book now / Buy now
- Any exclamation marks

## Words to Favour

- Considered
- Composed
- Restrained
- Unhurried
- Provenance
- Craftsmanship
- Quiet
- Enduring
